Once a patient has successfully received charity care, the ACT and referrals teams will notify the Head TS of the week, confirm that the referral is still necessary, and schedule the patient's appointment. For urgent referrals, Dr. Meah will draft a request to Radiology Associates with the EC.

If the patient does not have charity care, follow the necessary steps to obtain charity care.

Cost

Cost to EHHOP Patient: For patients with Charity Care Level 1, the CT scan will cost $20 per body part regardless of whether contrast is used, and which body part is scanned. To find out the costs for patients who are not Level 1, please contact the Radiology Referrals Manager.

Cost to Society: CT scans w/o contrast cost ~$500 in new york out of pocket.

Wait time

Once the patient has obtained charity care, it takes about a week to schedule a CT scan.
